# Northstar Healthcare Patient Flow

**Industry:** Healthcare operations  
**Customer footprint:** 5 acute-care hospitals, 2 specialty sites, 1 centralized transfer center  
**Program type:** Operational capacity management program with security and compliance scrutiny from day 1

## Customer Situation

Northstar Health was managing patient placement, discharge readiness, and transfer escalation through fragmented views in Epic, a bed-board tool, EVS updates, and staffing spreadsheets. Throughput leaders needed one operational command center that could show which patients were truly discharge-ready, where bed turnover was blocked, and what action would relieve capacity before the evening surge.

## Delivery Goal

Deliver a patient-flow command center that lets capacity nurses and transfer-center staff prioritize bed assignment, accelerate discharge barriers, and escalate interfacility transfers with auditable operational reasoning.

## Headline Outcome

The bootcamp-target scope aimed to reduce median bed assignment latency from 52 minutes to under 20 minutes during weekday discharge peaks while increasing transparency into environmental services, staffing, and discharge-barrier delays.

## Artifact Chain In This Example

This example includes the full discovery-through-retro artifact chain and shows a stricter compliance posture than the manufacturing example. It is especially useful for seeing how governance, PHI scoping, access control, and operator trust influence architecture and deployment decisions.

## What Makes This Example Useful

This engagement demonstrates a realistic healthcare deployment with PHI boundaries, shift-based operator workflows, escalation-heavy decision logic, and a mix of Workshop, agent, and OSDK surfaces for different clinical and operational audiences.
